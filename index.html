Transition to UWI MONA

September 30, 2022 by Kayla Swift

As a regional student, I had to undertake a challenging and unexpected journey from my own St. Vincent and the Grenadines to Jamacia. I had hoped to begin my classes in the face-to-face format after being accepted in 2021, but the covid epidemic prevented that. I was looking forward to assimilating into Jamaica's Jamaican culture and way of life. After arriving in Jamacia, I participated in all the hall’s activities since I wanted my whole First Year Experience (FYE), since my first year I was robbed. I stepped straight into the orientation process, learning everything I could about UWI and the background and organization of my hall, making friends, and thoroughly enjoying myself.
Numerous events were placed in the weeks before school started, and as I've already mentioned, I attended them all to fully participate. A fete and other freshmen competitions were held by the UWI guild. I also attended an event off campus. One of the things I took away from these things was that the Caribbean islands might be separated by the sea, but they are connected through experiences and cultures. I remember conversations I had with the other students, we were talking about doing the same CSEC subjects, and noting the similarities in the way our teachers delivered the material and our experience taking the exam it was the same. We also had a conversation about our school break traditions, going to the beach, playing in the road but making sure to it home before the streetlights come on. We are the same people.
